# Diagnostic Lenses

Initial lens map for the Abundance Systems Memo skill.

## Abundance Thesis

Use when the problem involves scarcity, blocked building, anti-growth defaults, or failure to produce enough of what people need.

Core diagnostic questions:

- What socially valuable thing is too hard, slow, expensive, or illegal to build?
- Which process claims to protect the public while preventing the public from getting the thing it needs?
- Where is the system optimizing for avoiding blame instead of producing outcomes?

## Why Institutions Get Clogged

Use when the problem involves procedural pileup, veto points, coordination failure, or an institution that appears unable to act even when many people agree something should happen.

Core diagnostic questions:

- Which steps were added to prevent a past failure, and what do they now block?
- Who can stop action without being responsible for the cost of stopping it?
- Where does the institution confuse process completion with public value?

## Government Execution And IT

Use when the problem involves procurement, service delivery, digital systems, benefits, eligibility, implementation, or state capacity.

Core diagnostic questions:

- Who owns the user experience end to end?
- Where are policy, procurement, operations, and software separated in ways that prevent learning?
- What feedback reaches decision-makers quickly enough to change implementation?

## Housing And Land Use

Use when the problem involves zoning, permitting, environmental review, infill, infrastructure, local veto points, or housing supply.

Core diagnostic questions:

- Which actor has the power to say no, delay, or demand concessions?
- Who captures the benefit of scarcity, and who pays the cost?
- Which rules make legal building diverge from what the community says it wants?

## Geographic Mobility And Opportunity

Use when the problem involves people being unable to move toward jobs, housing, education, safety, or opportunity.

Core diagnostic questions:

- What keeps people physically, legally, financially, or socially stuck?
- Which local constraints become national opportunity constraints?
- Where does policy assume mobility while the lived system prevents it?

## Historical Roots Of Proceduralism

Use when the problem involves public-interest procedure, litigation, participatory process, environmental review, administrative law, or reform movements that created durable constraints.

Core diagnostic questions:

- What historical abuse or failure was this procedure designed to prevent?
- Has the procedure become a generalized veto mechanism?
- Does the process still protect the public, or mostly empower organized incumbents?
