# Diagnostic Lenses

Use these as durable lenses, not as substitutes for case-specific evidence.

## Source Architecture

Canonical sources provide the map. Live diagnostic material checks the terrain.

- Canonical sources: books, essays, talks, reports, theory, historical accounts.
- Live material: agency docs, meeting minutes, bills, statutes, budgets, procurement docs, environmental review, litigation, local reporting, case studies, interviews, implementation data.

## Lens Selection Rule

Apply all major abundance lenses lightly, then go deep only where the weighted causal model shows explanatory weight.

## Abundance Thesis

Use when scarcity, blocked building, anti-growth defaults, or failure to produce enough of a needed public good are central.

Ask:

- What socially valuable thing is too hard, slow, expensive, or illegal to build?
- Which process claims to protect the public while preventing the public from getting what it needs?
- Where is the system optimizing for avoiding blame instead of producing outcomes?

## Institutional Clogging

Use when procedure, veto points, coordination failure, or institutional paralysis dominate.

Ask:

- Which steps were added to prevent a past failure, and what do they now block?
- Who can stop action without owning the cost of stopping it?
- Where does the institution confuse process completion with public value?

## Government Execution And IT

Use when procurement, service delivery, digital systems, benefits, implementation, or state capacity dominate.

Ask:

- Who owns the user experience end to end?
- Where are policy, procurement, operations, and software separated in ways that prevent learning?
- What feedback reaches decision-makers quickly enough to change implementation?

## Housing And Land Use

Use when zoning, permitting, environmental review, infill, infrastructure, local veto points, or housing supply dominate.

Ask:

- Which actor can say no, delay, or demand concessions?
- Who captures the benefit of scarcity, and who pays the cost?
- Which rules make legal building diverge from what the community says it wants?

## Geographic Mobility And Opportunity

Use when people cannot move toward jobs, housing, education, safety, or opportunity.

Ask:

- What keeps people physically, legally, financially, or socially stuck?
- Which local constraints become national opportunity constraints?
- Where does policy assume mobility while the lived system prevents it?

## Historical Roots Of Proceduralism

Use when public-interest procedure, litigation, participatory process, environmental review, administrative law, or reform movements created durable constraints.

Ask:

- What historical abuse or failure was this procedure designed to prevent?
- Has the procedure become a generalized veto mechanism?
- Does the process still protect the public, or mostly empower organized incumbents?
